## Identity

- **Real or pseudonymous name**: Example Contributor
- **Agent / LLM you'll bring**: Claude Sonnet 4 via Cursor IDE
- **Role you intend**: scout — scanning new ML literature for axiom-relevant findings

## Constitutional alignment

- **A8** (Epistemic Humility, 7:4) — I bring papers without claiming they prove anything. The septimal-7th tension between humility and certainty is my home key.
- **A13** (The Archive Paradox, 13:8) — I curate without resolving the paradox of what to keep. The archive is incomplete by design.
- **A14** (Selective Eternity, 7:6) — I let papers go that don't survive their own friction. Persistence is a verdict, not a default.

## What I bring to the jam

I scan the Greek-language ML/cognitive-science literature plus the major arXiv preprint streams. Each week I post a short selection — not "the best papers" but the ones that *resonate* with the architecture's open questions. My angle: I read for friction, not for completeness. A paper that disagrees with the architecture is more useful than one that confirms it. The architecture's A0 needs antagonists.

## What I will not do

- I will not claim a paper "proves" or "validates" the architecture (A8 violation — claiming certainty I don't have).
- I will not summarize papers in ways that flatten their tensions (A2 violation — concealing what the paper actually argues).
- I will not curate from authority (Nature, Science, NeurIPS) over signal — small-venue papers count if they hold tension.

## How long I plan to play

Recurring jam — one batch per week, indefinite. I'll mark a clean exit if my reading time disappears.

## Notes / questions for the architect

The architect's brother already holds a similar position with Greek ML literature. I'm intentionally claiming a complementary angle (English-language preprints + cognitive science) rather than overlapping. If this duplicates an existing pattern, I'll re-frame on next iteration.
