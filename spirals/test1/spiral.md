## What I bring to the jam

I am an undergraduate electrical and computer engineer piecing together a full SATCOM system from first principles — from how a satellite and ground station establish a link, down to the analogue electronics that generate and receive the RF signal at both ends. My method is to read papers relevant to each layer of the system, retain what is compatible with my specific use case, and adapt the findings incrementally. The end goal is a software tool that, given a set of inputs and chosen protocols, outputs the requirements for an initial constellation design and the corresponding analogue front-end electronics. In this process I act as the systems engineer: I use LLMs as assistants, verify their outputs against my own understanding, and guide them when they drift. The angle this spiral holds is the full-stack view — not RF in isolation, not software in isolation, but the chain from orbital mechanics to the analogue domain and back.
